The story of mamafaka is the website, created for paying tribute to Pharuephon "Tum" Mukdasanit that also known as "Mamafaka", and also be used as a source for gathering all of his information and artworks in order to pass the inspiration to the future generations.

The Begining

Mamafaka was born and raised in Khon Kaen. After finishing a vocational course, he joined Rangsit University, taking up graphic design ‘where he fell in love with art’, according to the Bangkok Post. In 2002, during his course at Rangsit University.

Mamafaka gathered five other design friends to establish a design group called ‘B.O.R.E.D’. As a result, most Thai graphic designers know him as ‘’Mamafaka B.O.R.E.D”. The group was involved in many events, from the Fat Festival to Tiger Translate, and was influential to street art, graphic design and pop-cultural in Bangkok.

He started career life at Ogilvy&Mather in 2004, then Creative juice in Bangkok as Graphic designer on the following year, He won award during his advertising career such as B.A.D award, Adsman, Adfest etc. However his advertising career was only but a brief one.

His artwork started to appear in quite numbers of famous local and international design magazine since then, such as Wallpaper*, Territory, CG+ etc. and also being the key designer who have been involved in famous project such as Tiger Translate, Bangkok design festival, For exhibition and talk of the town fixed gear event “Ride a Life”.

The Big Shift

The Turning Point

Mamafaka became a freelance designer in 2011, specializing on graphics design, street art and illustration. He then created the fictional character “Mr. Hellyeah!”.

The success of “Mr. Hellyeah!” proved that he would soon launch his own line of product. Mamafaka was on high demand, working with well-known brands: Converse, VANS, K-Swiss, Billabong, Preduce Skateboard, Smirnoff etc.In addition, he was still actively creating fine arts for galleries and exhibitions through Bangkok.

While on holiday in Phuket, Mamfaka reportedly had a surfing accident in Kata beach on the Saturday morning of 7th of September 2013. While being unconscious, he was rescued by lifeguards and was rushed to Patong hospital, later on moving to Bangkok Hospital Phuket.

After being in a coma for 48 hours, Mamfaka passed away on Monday, 9th of September 2013. The news was shocking to design and graphic circles all around Thailand. Mamfaka’s funeral was held at his birth province, Khon Kaen in the North-east of Thailand.

The Early Death

Although Mamafaka passed away for many years, his works still influence and inspire people in many ways. This is the reason for creating “Mamafaka Family” to be a memorial and establish in his honour. This will be a community for fans and people who are interest in about his stories. Moreover, there are activities and new products that will be launched all over the year. The updates will be announced in this website, and also social medias.
